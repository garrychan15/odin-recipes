Burger

Description

A burger, or hamburger, is a dish consisting of a cooked patty of ground meat or other savory ingredients, served in a sliced bun or roll. While the term "hamburger" historically implied a beef patty, "burger" is now a broader term for a wide range of patties made from ingredients like chicken, fish, vegetables, and plant-based alternatives.

Burgers are often garnished with toppings such as cheese, lettuce, tomato, and onion, and are typically accompanied by condiments like ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise.

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground beef (80% lean, 20% fat)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Â½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • 4 burger buns
  • Toppings: lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles
  • Sauces: ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise

Steps:

  1. Prepare the patties: Gently mix the beef, salt, pepper, and garlic in a bowl, being careful not to overmix, which can make the burgers tough. Form the mixture into four equal patties, about 2 cm thick. Press a small dimple into the center of each patty with your thumb to help it cook evenly.
  2. Cook the burgers: Heat the oil or butter in a pan or grill over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for 3-4 minutes per side for medium doneness. For a cheeseburger, add a slice of cheese in the last minute of cooking.
  3. Toast the buns: Lightly toast the buns on the pan or grill.
  4. Assemble and serve: Spread your preferred sauces on the buns. Layer with the burger patty and toppings.
